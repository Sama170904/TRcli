# Balanced Price Range (BPR)

## Definition
A Balanced Price Range (BPR) is created when **two opposing Fair Value Gaps (a bullish and a bearish FVG) overlap in the exact same price range**. 

This indicates that price has displaced aggressively in one direction and then immediately displaced back in the opposite direction through the same zone. This rapid two-way displacement balances the price delivery, and the overlapping range becomes a highly sensitive, high-probability support or resistance zone.

```
       [Bearish FVG]
     =================  <- Top of BPR
     |  Overlapping  |
     |     Range     |
     =================  <- Bottom of BPR
       [Bullish FVG]
```

## Core Principles
- **High-Probability Support/Resistance:** Because the range has been delivered rapidly in both directions, it is fully balanced. Any future retest of this zone has a very high probability of rejecting.
- **Magnetic Draw:** Price is naturally drawn to BPRs to retest them before continuing the real institutional expansion.
- **Polarity Flip:** A bullish BPR (where the bullish displacement was the second, stronger move) acts as support. A bearish BPR (where the bearish displacement was the second, stronger move) acts as resistance.
